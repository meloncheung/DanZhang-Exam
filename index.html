How will coronavirus impact the world economy?

Coronavirus (COVID-19), which originated in Wuhan, China, is still knocking the world now. It swept China first since January, then the West by the end of February and later Latin America. On 26th May, over 5 million people were infected globally with 350,000 fatalities due to the virus.

Beyond health concerns, the pandemic also has a significant impact on the global economy. Previous studies of epidemics such as H1N1 points out an epidemic would affect the economy negatively. Fear of infection and containment measures implimented to stop the transmission will disrupt regular economic activity and decreased trades.

Before heading into economic fallout, let us have a look at the current situation of coronavirus.

The situation seems severe as China sees a record blow with -6.8 GDP declined in the first quarter of 2020, and nearly a 13% fall compared to GDP in 2019. World economy may be worse. The International monetary fund (IMF) released its Economic outlook on coronavirus in April 2020. According to the IMF projection, China's economic growth in 2020 will be relatively positive, compared with other economically crippled countries.
